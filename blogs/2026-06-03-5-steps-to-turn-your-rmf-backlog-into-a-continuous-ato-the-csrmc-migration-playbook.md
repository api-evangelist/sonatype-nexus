The article explains how federal agencies can transition from legacy Risk Management Framework processes to the new Cybersecurity Risk Management Construct by automating compliance through continuous authorization. It outlines five strategies including deploying evidence engines, integrating security into development pipelines, governing AI-assisted code, implementing risk scoring systems, and achieving multi-agency reciprocity to eliminate manual compliance backlogs and transform static authorizations into a true Continuous ATO.
